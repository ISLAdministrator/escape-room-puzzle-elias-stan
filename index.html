Escape Room

You push open the door to the science lab and immediately feel something is wrong. The usual hum of equipment is louder, harsher — almost angry. On one of the benches, a sealed container glows faintly, pulsing in uneven bursts of light. Warning symbols flash on a nearby monitor: “REACTIVITY LEVELS UNSTABLE.” A log scrolls across the screen, filled with scientific notes about radiation and energy release. Some lines are highlighted, repeating the same idea over and over: rate, exposure, control. Whoever was here before didn’t finish their work. If the radiation keeps releasing energy this fast, something bad is going to happen. To stabilise the container, you’ll need to understand what’s causing the reaction — and how to slow it down.
